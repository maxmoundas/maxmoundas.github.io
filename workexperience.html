Vanderbilt University's Future of Learning and Generative AI Initiative

Our work focuses on leveraging large language models like ChatGPT to automate software development, training Vanderbilt students and faculty to incorporate generative AI tools into their work, and working on interdisciplinary projects with various departments at the university. As generative AI grows and evolves, so do its uses and best practices. We aim to make Vanderbilt University a leading organization in the field of large language models and prompt engineering.

I oversee a group of undergraduates and lead numerous projects that involve collaboration across different fields. We are currently working on building experimentation platforms to automate the testing and benchmarking of various prompt tactics, automated systems for software development and an argumentative paper to improve the documentation supplied by American Nursing Association.

Summer 2021

During my first summer internship with Aimbridge, I had the opportunity to work on a major project that had a significant impact on the company's internal communications system. Specifically, I developed a Dynamic Distribution Group system that uses PowerShell scripts alongside SQL jobs to automate internal communications within the organization. This system was critical in ensuring that Aimbridge's tens of thousands of employees, including senior executives, were receiving critical messages on a regular basis.

One of the key benefits of this project was the discovery and elimination of over $150,000 in annual unnecessary Microsoft license fees. By streamlining the communication process, we were able to reduce the number of licenses required to support the system, resulting in substantial cost savings for the company.

This project was a major success, eliminating communication issues and ensuring that Aimbridge's associates receive internal communications when expected. I am proud to have played a key role in this effort, and I believe that my experience with this project has helped me to develop a deep understanding of the importance of effective communication in the workplace. Summer 2022

During my second summer internship with Aimbridge, I had the opportunity to work on a project that had a significant impact on the company's license management process. I am proud to have played a key role in this effort, and I believe that my experience with this project has helped me to develop a deep understanding of the importance of effective license management and organization.

This project was a major success, and I am excited about the prospect of continuing to find new and innovative ways to improve business processes and optimize systems through the use of technology.
