Vanderbilt University's Institute for Software Integrated Systems: Generative AI Research Engineer

May 2023 - Present

After graduation, I accepted an opportunity to work alongside Jules White, the Associate Dean of Strategic Learning Programs, on Vanderbilt's recently launched Future of Learning and Generative AI initiative as a research engineer. Since I started in May 2023, I've taken a deep dive into the capabilities and practical applications of Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT and other generative AI tools. My journey and contributions can be summarized as follows:

  • Experimentation with LLMs: Through multiple experiments, I've explored the ability of LLMs to generate full-stack software applications, to accurately assess the runtime performance of different code samples, and to consistently produce desired text for automated systems.
  • Automated Incident Response Plan: In collaboration with Vanderbilt CISO, Shane Callahan, we've pioneered the development of an LLM-based automated incident response plan generator. This initiative is aimed at bolstering security responsiveness using generative AI.
  • Tutoring the University Chancellor: I have had the unique opportunity to personally educate and advise Vanderbilt Chancellor, Daniel Diermeier, on the potential and applications of LLMs and generative AI tools. This has ensured a top-down understanding of the transformative power of AI within the institution.
  • Experimentation Platforms: Recognizing the need for streamlined testing processes, I have spearheaded the development of experimentation platforms. These platforms play a critical role in automating the testing and benchmarking of various prompt engineering strategies.
  • Research and Publication: My curiosity has fueled various research papers, both as an author and co-author, where we delve deep into the potential of LLMs for streamlining and automating software development processes. My most recent paper on Prompt Engineering of ChatGPT to Improve Generated Code and Runtime Performance Compared with the Top-Voted Human Solutions was published in August 2023.
  • University Advising: I've taken on the responsibility of advising Vanderbilt University professors and faculty on the best practices for harnessing LLMs, especially in a teaching environment. This advising includes writing internal communications and research papers on the best applications of LLMs for educators.
  • Collaborative Ventures: My expertise has been sought across various departments, resulting in multidisciplinary projects. I've worked closely with teams at the Vanderbilt University Medical Center, the Vanderbilt University IT department, and projects associated with the National Security Agency to ensure that AI is used optimally across different sectors.
  • Team Leadership: As part of my responsibilities, I've had the privilege of managing a dynamic team of eight undergraduates. Their hard work and dedication has been extremely helpful in completing projects and expanding the scope of multiple ventures.

As generative AI grows and evolves, so do its uses and best practices. In every capacity, my aim remains the same: to harness the capabilities of generative AI for the betterment of our academic, research, and operational endeavors.
